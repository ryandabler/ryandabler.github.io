As a person,

I love solving problems and taking a deep dive into learning new things, so when I had to learn advanced SQL concepts to give a day-by-day running total of a company's inventory I knew I had to be a programmer. Since becoming one, I have not regretted that decision.

I do as much as I can with vanilla JavaScript because that gives me the best understanding of how browsers, frame­works, and the web work, although I happily work with frameworks to renew my sense of wonder for what can be done in the language.

When I am not programming, I read literature and philo­sophy, make sauerkraut, and walk around town.
